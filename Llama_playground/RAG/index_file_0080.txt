What caused the conflict in Sudan and what is its impact on the region?
The conflict in Sudan began as an escalating power struggle between the two main factions of the military regime and has its roots in the violence and human rights abuses in Darfur about 20 years ago. The conflict has caused one of the worst humanitarian crises in recent history, triggering the world’s largest hunger crisis and creating the world’s worst displacement crisis. It has also affected Sudan’s neighbouring countries, with millions of Sudanese refugees fleeing to Chad and South Sudan. The conflict has attracted regional power plays, complicating the chances of a successful transition to civilian-led government.
