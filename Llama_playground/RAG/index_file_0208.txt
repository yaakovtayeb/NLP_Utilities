What inspired Amina Agueznay to weave an artwork from palm husks and what did she win for it?

At 2024-feb-28, amina Agueznay was inspired to weave an artwork from palm husks during a workshop in Morocco’s Souss-Massa region, as part of a project with local rug weavers to renovate Tissekmoudine, a ksar, or fortified village. She won the Norval Sovereign African art prize, worth $35,000 (£28,000), for her winning work, Portal #1, which is reminiscent of a jagged-edge motif found on Moroccan rugs and inspired by the symbols painted on the doors of a ksar.
