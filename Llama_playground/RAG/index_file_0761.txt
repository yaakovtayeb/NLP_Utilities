Who was Ebrahim Golestan and what were his contributions to Iranian film-making?

At 2023-sep-24, ebrahim Golestan was a leading intellectual and writer in Iran who worked in public relations for a western oil consortium in 1954. He founded his own Golestan Studios in 1957 and became the most sophisticated centre for film-making in Iran. He produced several films, including A Fire (1961) and Brick and Mirror (1964). He also worked with the feminist poet Forugh Farrokhzad and produced her documentary The House Is Black (1963). After Farrokhzad's death, Golestan opted for exile and sold his studio. He made his permanent home in Britain in the 1970s.
