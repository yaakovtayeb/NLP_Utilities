What are the environmental risks posed by the sinking of Rubymar in the Red Sea?

At 2024-mar-05, the sinking of Rubymar in the Red Sea poses grave environmental risks due to the thousands of tonnes of fertiliser that threaten to spill into the sea, which could harm marine life, including coral reefs, and affect coastal communities that rely on fishing for their livelihoods. The leaked fuel and chemical pollutant could also cause damage to the environment.
